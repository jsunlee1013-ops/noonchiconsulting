AI Literacy Programs for Educational Leaders & Educators

Choose Your Professional Development Path

Two Specialized Tracks

We offer distinct programs designed for different roles in educational organizations. Each integrates proven national AI education standards to ensure comprehensive, practical learning.

Executive Leadership Track

For Superintendents, Assistant Superintendents, Chief Officers, School Board Members

Instructional Applications Track

For Teachers, Instructional Coaches, Curriculum Specialists, Principals

Executive Leadership Track

Strategic AI Decision-Making for District Leaders

Duration: 8 weeks | Format: Weekly 2-hour executive sessions | Group Size: 8-12 senior leaders

What You'll Master

Week 1-2: Strategic Foundation
  • Understand AI capabilities for informed strategic planning
  • Assess your organization's readiness for AI integration
  • Develop executive vocabulary for AI discussions

Why This Matters: You need foundational knowledge to make million-dollar technology decisions and lead organizational change confidently.

Week 3-4: Policy & Governance
  • Create comprehensive AI policies for district-wide use
  • Navigate legal requirements and ethical considerations
  • Establish oversight structures for responsible AI adoption

Why This Matters: Without clear policies, AI use becomes chaotic and potentially risky. Boards and communities expect thoughtful governance.

Week 5-6: Procurement & Investment
  • Evaluate AI vendors with informed, technical criteria
  • Calculate true costs and return on investment
  • Negotiate contracts that protect your district's interests

Why This Matters: AI purchases represent significant budget commitments. Poor vendor selection wastes money and undermines educational goals.

Week 7-8: Change Leadership
  • Design organization-wide AI implementation strategies
  • Communicate AI initiatives to all stakeholder groups
  • Create professional development systems for sustained adoption

Why This Matters: Technology only succeeds when people embrace it. Your leadership determines whether AI enhances or disrupts your organization.

Standards Integration

  • National AI Education Framework (AI4K12): Provides technical understanding for informed decision-making
  • Educational Technology Standards (ISTE): Guides organizational change and professional development planning
  • Computer Science Education Standards (CSTA): Ensures procurement decisions meet actual educational technology needs

Executive Outcomes

  • ✅ Make confident AI investment decisions
  • ✅ Create policies that balance innovation with protection
  • ✅ Lead organizational change that embraces AI responsibly
  • ✅ Communicate AI strategies effectively to boards and communities

Instructional Applications Track

Practical AI Integration for Teaching & Learning

Duration: 12 weeks | Format: Weekly 90-minute workshops | Group Size: 12-15 educators

What You'll Master

Week 1-2: AI Literacy Foundations
  • Develop personal AI understanding to guide student learning
  • Recognize AI systems and their educational applications
  • Create age-appropriate AI learning experiences for students

Why This Matters: You can't teach what you don't understand. Students need AI literacy, and you're their guide.

Week 3-4: AI Teaching Assistant
  • Use AI tools to enhance lesson planning and content creation
  • Streamline administrative tasks while maintaining teaching authenticity
  • Develop efficient workflows that improve instruction quality

Why This Matters: AI can save hours of preparation time, letting you focus on what matters most - connecting with students.

Week 5-6: Student Creativity & Expression
  • Guide students in using AI as creative partner, not replacement
  • Design assignments that leverage AI while requiring original thinking
  • Foster student innovation enhanced by AI capabilities

Why This Matters: Students will use AI throughout their lives. Teaching them to collaborate creatively with AI is essential preparation.

Week 7-8: Assessment & Feedback
  • Implement AI tools that provide meaningful student feedback
  • Design evaluations that work effectively alongside AI capabilities
  • Use AI analytics to identify learning gaps and student needs

Why This Matters: In an AI world, assessment must evolve. You need strategies that measure authentic learning, not AI output.

Week 9-10: Ethics & Academic Integrity
  • Develop classroom policies for responsible AI use
  • Teach students ethical decision-making about AI assistance
  • Create environments that encourage honesty about AI use

Why This Matters: Academic integrity doesn't disappear with AI - it evolves. Students need clear guidance about responsible use.

Week 11-12: Future-Ready Learning
  • Prepare students for AI-integrated careers and citizenship
  • Develop personal professional learning plans for ongoing AI growth
  • Create sustainable practices for evolving with AI technology

Why This Matters: Today's students will live and work in an AI-integrated world. Your teaching shapes their readiness for that future.

Standards Integration

  • National AI Education Framework (AI4K12): Provides curriculum goals for student AI literacy development
  • Educational Technology Standards (ISTE): Guides effective technology integration and student empowerment strategies
  • Computer Science Education Standards (CSTA): Ensures students develop appropriate AI competencies for their grade level

Educator Outcomes

  • ✅ Integrate AI tools seamlessly into existing curriculum
  • ✅ Teach students responsible AI use and digital citizenship
  • ✅ Design learning experiences that leverage AI while maintaining academic rigor
  • ✅ Assess student learning effectively in an AI-enhanced environment

Why Standards Matter

Proven Framework

Our programs integrate three nationally recognized AI education standards, ensuring you receive comprehensive, research-based professional development rather than trend-following training.

Practical Application

Standards aren't academic theory - they're practical guides that help you make informed decisions about AI tools, policies, and classroom practices.

Future-Proof Learning

By understanding the foundational principles behind AI education, you're prepared for whatever new tools emerge, not just today's specific applications.

Professional Credibility

Standards-based professional development empowers you to lead confidently when others hesitate, positioning you as the go-to expert others turn to for AI guidance.
